Books & Authorship

For many years, I have known that one day I would find my voice as an author. It has taken time, trust, channeling, and plenty of "elbow grease," but the finish line is in sight. My book, The Third Layer, is currently in editing. It is a moving, modern story of transformation. My goal has always been to create a work that offers courage, insight, and the unwavering belief that change is possible. I am so incredibly excited to share it with you when it is ready. Until then, here is a small excerpt from the prologue:

“…I have decided to tell my story. In many ways, it differs from the lives of most people here in Denmark. Social control made it impossible to feel love without splitting myself apart—without being divided. The strings my soul played upon became frayed; they produced a cold, harsh sound every time they were struck by the 'reality' of social control. My soul bled so much that, for long periods, I felt hollow and toneless inside. But when I listened inward—when I took myself seriously and allowed my love to flow—the note of freedom returned with a clear, fine resonance, and the desire to play the instrument of life began to bloom once more.

But how do I tell a story of betrayal, grief, and psychological brutality without hurting or criticizing those involved? How do I speak of breakdowns and doubt without appearing weak? How do I discuss fundamentalism and religion without fueling hostility? Is it even possible?

Villains come in many disguises. Some even appear loving. They come in well-meaning forms, explaining exactly where you are wrong. Their own fear becomes an argument for preaching lies, creating a universe that children grow up in and adopt as their own reality.

For me, what I was raised to believe did not align with what I felt—and then what? I was confused. For the first thirty years of my life, my body, my soul, and my mind did not fit together. In a way, everything I had been taught made sense to my head, but it made no sense to my body or my soul. I realized that thoughts are merely the language of the brain. Thoughts are the mind's way of trying to find 'head or tail' in things; they are the yardstick for what we think we should do. But feelings are the language of the body. It is in our feelings that true understanding takes root. They are the barometer that tells you what you truly want. You can learn to believe in many things, but I have learned that you cannot force yourself to live by your thoughts alone. It simply doesn't work if the body, soul, and emotions are not on board. You become ill; you lose your focus. At least, I did. But I have also learned that you can change your entire foundation. You can grow wiser. You can learn anew. You can transform traumas from open wounds into useful experiences. And above all, you can reconnect with something you have known deep down all along. ”
